# Meridian Technologies: Our Values and Team Charter

**Living Our Values Through Change**

---

## Who We Are

Meridian Technologies was founded on the belief that technology should solve real problems for real people. Twelve years later, that belief still drives everything we do. Our values are not words on a wall; they are commitments we make to each other, to our customers, and to the communities where we operate.

During times of organizational change, values matter more than ever. They provide stability when structures shift, guide decisions when playbooks do not exist, and remind us why we do this work in the first place.

This charter describes our six core values and what they look like in practice, especially during periods of transition.

---

## Our Core Values

### 1. Build Trust Through Transparency

We earn trust by sharing information honestly, acknowledging what we do not know, and following through on commitments.

**During change:** Leaders communicate decisions openly. Managers are honest about what remains undecided. Employees feel safe asking direct questions.

### 2. Respect Every Voice

Every person brings a perspective that matters. We create environments where people speak up and disagree constructively regardless of title or tenure.

**During change:** Feedback channels remain active. We pay attention to voices quieted by uncertainty. No one is penalized for expressing concerns.

### 3. Own Your Impact

We take responsibility for our work, our decisions, and their consequences. Ownership means following through and fixing problems rather than passing them along.

**During change:** Managers support their teams through transitions directly. Employees take initiative to learn new processes. We measure success by impact on people, not just the org chart.

### 4. Grow Together

We invest in each other's development because individual growth and organizational growth are inseparable. Learning is part of the job.

**During change:** Development budgets are maintained, not cut. Managers discuss career paths in the new structure. We view reorganization as a development opportunity.

### 5. Put People First

Our business exists because of the people who build it, sell it, support it, and use it. We consider human impact alongside business impact.

**During change:** Affected employees receive comprehensive transition support. Well-being resources expand during uncertainty. We monitor the emotional impact of changes through check-ins and surveys.

### 6. Deliver With Integrity

We do what we say we will do. We set realistic expectations, meet commitments, and communicate early when circumstances change.

**During change:** Timelines are realistic. Commitments to employees are honored without exception. If plans change, employees hear first.

---

## Our Commitment During This Transition

Restructurings test values more than any normal operating period. Here is what we commit to:

1. **No surprises.** Affected employees will be notified directly and privately before any public announcement.
2. **Honest timelines.** We will publish realistic milestones and update them if they change.
3. **Continued investment.** Development budgets, wellness programs, and employee support will be maintained or expanded.
4. **Open feedback.** Multiple channels for employee feedback will remain active, and leadership will respond to the themes raised.
5. **Accountability.** We will conduct a post-transition review in September 2026 and share the results company-wide, including what we got wrong.

---

## Living the Charter

This charter is not a policy to be enforced. It is a shared agreement about how we treat each other. Every employee, from the newest hire to the CEO, is accountable to these values. If you see behavior that contradicts this charter, you have both the right and the responsibility to raise it.

The reorganization will change our structure. It will not change who we are.
