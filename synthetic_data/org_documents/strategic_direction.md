# Meridian Technologies: Strategic Direction 2026-2028

**Prepared by the Office of the CEO**
**March 2026**

---

## Our Context

The technology industry is undergoing a fundamental shift. Customers no longer want to purchase individual software products and assemble their own solutions. They want integrated platforms that solve complete business problems. Our largest competitors recognized this shift two years ago and reorganized around customer outcomes. We must do the same, and we must do it while preserving the engineering excellence and customer relationships that define Meridian.

This strategic direction document explains why we are reorganizing, where we are investing, and how every employee fits into the path forward.

## Market Pressures

Three forces are driving our decision:

**1. Customer consolidation.** Our customers are reducing vendor count. Our average enterprise customer used 4.2 Meridian products in 2024; by late 2025, that dropped to 3.1. They want fewer, deeper relationships, but our siloed structure makes bundled solutions difficult.

**2. Competitive acceleration.** Three of our top five competitors completed platform-oriented reorganizations in 2024-2025. Their integrated approach is winning deals we used to split among specialists.

**3. Talent expectations.** Our survey data shows that our best people want broader career paths and cross-functional collaboration. Our siloed structure limits mobility and creates frustration.

## Investment Areas

The reorganization creates the structure; our investment strategy fills it with purpose. Over the next two years, Meridian will focus on three growth areas:

**Integrated platform development.** Product Innovation (the new combined R&D and Platform Engineering unit) will receive a 15% budget increase to build connective infrastructure between our existing products. The goal is not to build a monolithic platform but to create seamless data flows and shared user experiences across our product suite.

**Customer success expansion.** Customer Success (the new combined Support and Client Relations unit) will hire 40 additional customer success managers in 2026. These roles are designed to be proactive partners for our customers, not reactive support agents. We believe that customer retention and expansion are our most efficient growth levers.

**Employee development.** We are committing $2 million in additional professional development funding for 2026, focused on cross-functional skills that align with our new structure. This includes technical training for customer-facing employees, business acumen programs for engineers, and leadership development for newly promoted managers.

## Growth Targets

| Metric | 2025 Actual | 2026 Target | 2028 Target |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Revenue | $420M | $470M | $580M |
| Customer retention | 87% | 91% | 94% |
| Employee engagement score | 3.4 / 5.0 | 3.8 / 5.0 | 4.2 / 5.0 |
| Internal mobility rate | 8% | 14% | 20% |
| Cross-sell attach rate | 22% | 35% | 50% |

These targets are ambitious but grounded in the operational improvements we expect from the reorganization. They are not aspirational statements; they are commitments that our leadership team will be measured against.

## How You Fit In

Every employee at Meridian plays a role in this strategy, and we do not say that as a corporate platitude. The reorganization was designed to remove barriers that prevented you from doing your best work. Here is what that means in practice:

- **Engineers:** You will work more closely with customers and customer success teams. Your insights about what is technically possible will directly shape product direction.
- **Customer-facing teams:** You will have a single, unified support structure behind you instead of navigating multiple internal teams. Your ability to solve customer problems end-to-end will improve.
- **Corporate teams:** You will support four business units instead of six, with standardized processes that reduce duplication and free up your time for higher-value work.
- **Managers:** You will have broader teams and more diverse talent. We are investing in your development to match the increased scope of your roles.

## Our Commitment

We will measure ourselves against these targets publicly. Quarterly business reviews will be shared company-wide. This transition will take 18 to 24 months to fully realize. We are asking for your engagement, your ideas, and your patience. In return, we commit to earning your trust through consistent, transparent action.
