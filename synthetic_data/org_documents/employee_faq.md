# Frequently Asked Questions: Meridian Technologies Reorganization

**Last Updated: March 20, 2026**
**Source: Office of the CEO and Human Resources**

---

This document answers common questions since the restructuring announcement on March 15, 2026. Submit additional questions through the anonymous portal on the intranet.

---

**Q1: Why is Meridian restructuring?**

Our industry is evolving toward integrated customer solutions, and our six-unit structure creates silos that slow collaboration. This reorganization positions us for better customer outcomes and more connected career paths. It is proactive, not a reaction to financial problems.

**Q2: Is my job at risk?**

Approximately 4% of positions will be eliminated where consolidation creates redundancy. If your role is affected, you will be notified by your manager and HR Business Partner no later than April 15, 2026.

**Q3: Will my compensation change?**

No. Base pay, bonus targets, and equity vesting schedules are not affected. If your role changes significantly, any compensation adjustment will follow the normal review process.

**Q4: Will my benefits change?**

Core benefits (health insurance, dental, vision, 401k matching, life insurance) are not changing. A separate benefits update will be sent in April regarding a new wellness program and minor adjustments to PTO accrual for employees crossing business unit boundaries. See the Benefits Update document for details.

**Q5: When will I know my new reporting line?**

The new organizational structure will be published on April 1, 2026. Your current manager will discuss changes with you before the public announcement.

**Q6: What if I disagree with my new team assignment?**

Have an open conversation with your new manager. If your skills are better suited to a different team, express interest through the internal mobility portal (refreshed in April). Transfer requests will be reviewed within 30 days.

**Q7: Will the restructuring affect our hybrid work policy?**

The core hybrid policy (minimum three days in office for most roles) is not changing. However, your specific in-office days may shift as you join a new team with different collaboration patterns. Your new manager will work with you to establish a schedule that works for both of you.

**Q8: What support is available if my position is eliminated?**

Every affected employee will receive:
- A minimum of 8 weeks of severance pay (more for longer-tenured employees)
- 90 days of continued health insurance coverage
- Access to an outplacement firm for career coaching and job search support
- Internal priority consideration for open positions in the new structure
- Access to the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) for counseling and support

**Q9: Can I apply for roles in the new structure?**

Yes. All new or restructured roles will be posted on the internal mobility portal before they are posted externally. Current employees will receive priority consideration. We encourage you to explore roles in the new structure that align with your skills and career goals.

**Q10: How will performance reviews work during the transition?**

Mid-year reviews proceed on schedule in June. If you change managers, your previous manager will provide a written summary to your new manager. No one will be disadvantaged because of the transition.

**Q11: What is happening to the specific business units?**

- **Enterprise Software + Professional Services** are combining into **Enterprise Solutions**
- **R&D + Platform Engineering** are combining into **Product Innovation**
- **Customer Support + Client Relations** are combining into **Customer Success**
- **Corporate Operations** (Finance, HR, Legal, IT) remains consolidated with minor adjustments

**Q12: Will there be layoffs beyond the 4%?**

We do not anticipate additional reductions. If business conditions change, we will communicate openly and early.

**Q13: How can I share feedback about the restructuring process?**

Several channels are available:
- **Anonymous question portal** on the intranet (responses posted weekly)
- **Company-wide Q&A sessions** on March 22 and March 29 with the CEO
- **Skip-level meetings** with your second-level manager (schedule through your manager)
- **HR office hours** every Wednesday, 2:00 to 3:00 PM
- **Direct email** to restructuring-feedback@meridiantech.com

**Q14: What is the timeline for the full transition?**

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| Restructuring announced | March 15, 2026 |
| New org chart published | April 1, 2026 |
| Affected employees notified | By April 15, 2026 |
| Transition period begins | May 1, 2026 |
| New structure fully operational | July 1, 2026 |
| Post-transition review | September 2026 |

**Q15: Who can I talk to if I am feeling anxious or stressed about these changes?**

- **Employee Assistance Program (EAP):** Confidential counseling available 24/7 at 1-800-555-0199
- **Manager check-ins:** Your manager is encouraged to discuss how you are feeling
- **Peer support:** ERGs are hosting drop-in sessions throughout March and April
- **HR Business Partners:** Available for confidential conversations about your situation

We are committed to honest communication throughout this process. If we are falling short, please tell us.
