# Meridian Technologies: Benefits Update for 2026

**From: Human Resources**
**Date: April 1, 2026**
**Effective: May 1, 2026 (unless otherwise noted)**

---

## Overview

As part of our ongoing commitment to employee well-being, we are making several updates to our benefits program for 2026. This update is separate from the organizational restructuring; these changes were planned before the reorganization was announced and reflect our annual benefits review process.

The guiding principle is simple: we are adding new benefits and adjusting existing ones to better support how our employees actually work and live. Nothing is being taken away.

## What Is Changing

### New: Meridian Wellness Program

Beginning May 1, we are launching a comprehensive wellness program available to all full-time and part-time employees working 20 or more hours per week.

The program includes:

- **Annual wellness stipend of $600** for fitness memberships, wellness apps, ergonomic equipment, or other health-related expenses. Receipts submitted through the existing expense system.
- **Mental health support expansion.** Our EAP provider is adding unlimited text-based counseling in addition to the existing 8 sessions of in-person or video counseling per year. This is fully confidential and available to all employees and their household members.
- **Quarterly wellness days.** Four additional paid days off per year (one per quarter), designated as wellness days. These are separate from PTO and do not carry over. The first wellness day is scheduled for June 13, 2026.
- **On-site health screenings** at our three largest office locations, starting in Q3 2026.

We are investing in wellness because our employee survey data shows that work-life balance and mental health support are among the top concerns across the organization. We believe that supporting your well-being is not separate from supporting your performance; they are the same thing.

### Adjusted: PTO Accrual for Cross-Unit Transfers

Employees who transfer between business units as part of the reorganization will retain their full PTO balance and accrual rate. Previously, cross-unit transfers reset the accrual clock. This policy change is retroactive to January 1, 2026, and applies to all internal transfers going forward.

### Adjusted: 401(k) Enrollment

Effective July 1, 2026, new hires will be auto-enrolled in the 401(k) plan at a 4% contribution rate (currently 3%). Existing employees' contribution rates are not affected. You may adjust your contribution at any time through the benefits portal. The company match remains unchanged at 50% of the first 6% you contribute.

### New: Dependent Care Flexible Spending Account (DCFSA) Increase

The annual DCFSA maximum is increasing from $5,000 to $5,500 for 2026, reflecting updated IRS limits. If you are currently enrolled, your maximum will be adjusted automatically. If you are not enrolled, the next open enrollment period is November 2026.

## What Is Not Changing

The following benefits remain exactly as they are:

- **Health insurance:** All medical, dental, and vision plans continue with the same carriers, coverage levels, and employee contribution rates
- **401(k) company match:** 50% match on the first 6% of salary contributed
- **Life insurance:** 2x annual salary basic life insurance, with voluntary supplemental options
- **Disability coverage:** Short-term and long-term disability insurance at current levels
- **Parental leave:** 16 weeks paid parental leave for all new parents
- **Education assistance:** Up to $5,250 per year for approved courses and degree programs
- **Commuter benefits:** Pre-tax commuter spending accounts for transit and parking
