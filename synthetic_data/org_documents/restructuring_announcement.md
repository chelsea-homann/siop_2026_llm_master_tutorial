# Meridian Technologies: A New Chapter

**From the Desk of Sarah Chen, CEO**
**Date: March 15, 2026**

---

Dear Meridian Team,

I am writing to share an important update about the future of Meridian Technologies. After extensive review by our leadership team and Board of Directors, we have made the decision to restructure our organization from six business units to four, effective Q2 through Q3 of this year.

## Why We Are Making This Change

Over the past two years, our industry has shifted significantly. Our customers are asking for integrated solutions rather than point products, and our competitors are moving faster because they are organized around customer outcomes rather than internal product lines. Our current six-unit structure, which served us well during our growth phase, now creates duplication of effort, slows decision-making, and makes it harder for our teams to collaborate across boundaries.

This is not a response to financial distress. Meridian is profitable and growing. This change is about positioning ourselves for the next stage of growth by removing internal barriers that slow us down.

## What Is Changing

We will consolidate from six business units to four:

- **Enterprise Solutions** (combining Enterprise Software and Professional Services)
- **Product Innovation** (combining R&D and Platform Engineering)
- **Customer Success** (combining Customer Support and Client Relations)
- **Corporate Operations** (Finance, HR, Legal, and IT will remain consolidated)

Some teams will merge, some reporting lines will change, and some roles will be restructured. I want to be direct: a small number of positions will be eliminated where consolidation creates genuine redundancy. We expect this to affect approximately 4% of our workforce. Every affected employee will receive a comprehensive transition package, and we will do everything in our power to support them.

## What Is Not Changing

Your compensation, benefits, and core employment terms are not affected by this restructuring. Our commitment to our hybrid work policy remains intact. Our values as a company remain the same.

## Timeline

- **April 2026:** New organizational structure announced with reporting lines
- **May 2026:** Transition period begins; teams start integrating
- **July 2026:** New structure fully operational
- **September 2026:** Post-transition review and adjustment

## What I Need From You

I need your patience, your questions, and your trust. Restructurings are difficult, and I will not pretend otherwise. What I can promise is transparency: you will hear from us regularly, and we will answer your questions honestly. If we do not have an answer yet, we will say so.

Your direct managers will schedule team meetings over the next two weeks to discuss what this means for your specific group. An FAQ document is available on the intranet, and we will hold company-wide Q&A sessions on March 22 and March 29.

I believe this change will make Meridian a stronger, more agile, and more rewarding place to work. But I also know that belief alone is not enough. We need to earn your confidence through our actions over the coming months.

Thank you for your dedication to Meridian. That dedication is what makes this company worth fighting for.

Sarah Chen
Chief Executive Officer
Meridian Technologies
